Culture and Track Record

Founding and Early Years

Tesla was founded in 2003 by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning and was named after inventor Nikola Tesla. Elon Musk became involved as chairman in 2004 and later took over as CEO. The company released its first electric car, the Roadster, in 2008, which was notable for its impressive range and performance[1][5].

Future Opportunities

Expansion of the Vehicle Lineup

Tesla plans to start production of a new mass market electric vehicle codenamed "Redwood" in mid-2025, which is expected to be a compact crossover[6].

The Future of Tesla Bot, Optimus

Optimus, also known as Tesla Bot, is a general-purpose robotic humanoid under development by Tesla. It is designed to perform tasks that are dangerous, repetitive, or boring. Elon Musk has stated that Optimus could be production-ready by 2023 and could be more significant than Tesla's vehicle business[4].
